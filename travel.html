Lesson 1

Word Skills: Titles, Headings & Borders

Follow these step-by-step instructions to format your travel guide in Microsoft Word.

✏️ Skill A: Creating a Colourful Title

Your title is the first thing people see. Make it big, bold, and eye-catching!

📄 Microsoft Word
B Bold
Font Size: 28
A Colour
Centre Align
Discover Barcelona
A Travel Guide by Sophie M.
1
Type your destination name

At the top of your Word document, type the name of your dream destination (e.g. "Discover Barcelona" or "Explore Tokyo").

2
Select the title text

Triple-click on the title to select it all, or use Ctrl + A if it's the only text.

3
Make it big — Font Size 28

In the Home tab, find the font size box. Click it and type 28. Press Enter.

4
Make it bold

Press Ctrl + B to make the title bold.

5
Add colour

Click the Font Colour dropdown arrow (the A with a coloured line under it). Choose a colour that matches your destination — green for forests, blue for beaches!

6
Centre align

Press Ctrl + E to centre your title on the page.

🔤 Skill B: Adding Sub-headings

Sub-headings break your guide into sections. They should be smaller than the title but still stand out.

Sub-headings you'll need: "Why Visit?", "5-Day Itinerary", "Top Tips", "Packing List" — each one gets the same formatting so your guide looks consistent.
1
Type your sub-heading

On a new line, type a section name like Why Visit?

2
Select it and set size to 16

Highlight the text and change the font size to 16.

3
Bold and underline

Press Ctrl + B for bold, then Ctrl + U for underline.

4
Optional: Add colour

Use the same colour as your title to keep things consistent. This makes your guide look polished.

Keyboard Shortcuts Cheat Sheet Ctrl+B = Bold | Ctrl+U = Underline | Ctrl+E = Centre | Ctrl+S = Save

🖼️ Skill C: Adding a Page Border

A page border frames your entire travel guide and makes it look like a real brochure.

1
Go to the Design tab

Click the Design tab at the top of Word (next to Layout).

2
Click "Page Borders"

On the right side of the ribbon, click Page Borders. A new window will open.

3
Choose a border style

In the Page Border window, choose Box on the left side. Then pick a style (try the double line or 3D effect). You can also choose a colour and width.

4
Try Art borders!

At the bottom of the window, there's an Art dropdown — this has fun decorative borders like palm trees, stars, and waves. Perfect for a travel guide!

Watch out! Don't use too many different colours or the border will clash with your guide. Pick one or two colours that match your theme.

🔍 What's Wrong With This?

Click each card to reveal the formatting mistake and learn how to fix it.

🖼️
A huge image that takes up the entire page with no text visible
Fix: Right-click the image → Size and Position → set width to about 8-10cm. Also use text wrapping so words flow around it.
📊
A table with no borders, no headings, and all the text is tiny
Fix: Select the table → Table Design tab → choose a border style. Add bold headers in the first row. Use size 11 or 12 for table text.
📝
A text box with no border, white background, and the text is the same as normal text
Fix: Click the text box → Shape Format → add a coloured border and light background fill. Make the text bold so it stands out as a tip.
📋
A packing list written as one long sentence: "bring sun cream and shorts and..."
Fix: Put each item on its own line and use bullet points (Home tab → Bullets). This makes the list easy to scan quickly.

Lesson 2

Word Skills: Images, Tables & Text Boxes

Follow these step-by-step instructions to add the final pieces to your travel guide.

📸 Skill A: Inserting & Formatting Images

Images make your travel guide come alive! You need at least 2 images with captions.

1
Find images online

Open a new browser tab and search for photos of your destination (e.g. "Barcelona beach"). Right-click and Save Image As to your computer.

2
Insert the image in Word

Click where you want the image. Go to Insert tab → Pictures → This Device. Find your saved image and click Insert.

3
Resize the image

Click on the image and drag the corner handles to make it smaller. Hold Shift while dragging to keep the proportions correct.

4
Set text wrapping

Click the image → click the Layout Options icon (it appears next to the image) → choose Square so text wraps around it.

5
Add a caption

Click below the image and type a caption in italic (use Ctrl + I). Example: "The beautiful beaches of Barcelona"

Top Tip! Use at least 2 images in your guide — one near the top and one further down. This breaks up the text and makes it more interesting to read.

📊 Skill B: Creating a 5-Day Itinerary Table

Tables are perfect for organising your itinerary. Here's how to create one:

📄 Microsoft Word — Table Example
Insert → Table
5 rows × 4 columns
DayActivityCostNotes
Day 1Arrive and exploreFreeCheck into hotel
Day 2Beach and water sports£15Bring sun cream!
Day 3Cultural tour£10Wear comfy shoes
Day 4Adventure day trip£25Book in advance
Day 5Shopping and departureVariesGreat for souvenirs
1
Insert a table

Click where you want the table. Go to Insert tab → Table → drag to select 4 columns × 6 rows (1 header + 5 days).

2
Type the headers

In the first row, type: Day, Activity, Cost, Notes. Make these bold (Ctrl + B).

3
Fill in the rows

In each row, type the day number, what activity you'd do, how much it costs, and any notes or tips.

4
Format the header row

Select the entire first row. Go to Table Design tab → Shading → choose a dark colour. Change the text to white using Font Colour.

5
Add borders

Select the whole table. Go to Table Design → Borders → choose All Borders to make grid lines visible.

💡 Skill C: Creating a "Top Tip" Text Box

Text boxes are great for callout tips, fun facts, or important warnings that you want to stand out.

1
Insert a text box

Go to Insert tab → Text Box → choose Simple Text Box (or draw your own).

2
Type your tip

Delete the placeholder text and type something like: "Top Tip: Book your tickets online to skip the queues!"

3
Add a border and fill

Click the text box border → Shape Format tab → Shape Outline (choose a colour) → Shape Fill (choose a light pastel colour).

4
Position the text box

Click and drag the text box to position it next to relevant content. Resize it by dragging the corner handles.

📋 Skill D: Creating Bullet Point Lists

Bullet points are perfect for packing lists, top tips, or "things to do" sections.

1
Type each item on a new line

Press Enter after each item to start a new line.

2
Select all your items

Highlight all the lines you want to turn into a bullet list.

3
Click the Bullets button

In the Home tab, find the Bullets button (it looks like a small list with dots). Click the dropdown arrow to choose a bullet style.
