📋 So… what even is a CV?

A CV stands for Curriculum Vitae — a Latin phrase that means "the story of your life." Don't worry about the Latin! Think of it simply as a one or two page document about you.

When you apply for a job, work experience, or a college course, you send someone your CV. It's usually the first thing an employer reads about you — before they even meet you. So it needs to look good and say the right things!

Think of it like this… Your CV is like a trailer for a film. It needs to make people want to find out more about you. If the trailer is boring, they won't bother watching the film!

🗂️ The 5 Key Sections of a CV

Most CVs have the same sections — like chapters in a book. Here they are:

1
Personal Details Your name, phone number and email. No photo needed yet — we'll add one in Word!
2
Personal Profile 3–4 sentences about who you are, what you enjoy and what you're like as a person. This is the most personal part of your CV.
3
Key Skills A bullet point list of your top strengths — like teamwork, communication, or being good with computers.
4
Education Your school name, the subjects you study, and any grades or predicted grades.
5
Hobbies & Interests Things you do outside school — sports, art, volunteering, gaming. This shows your personality!

Setting Up Your CV in Word

Open Word and follow these steps to create your CV document — we'll do it together.

📘 Word Skill: Page Layout Before you type anything, set up your page correctly. A CV needs narrow margins so you have more space.

📐 Step 1 — Set Your Page Margins

Margins are the empty space around the edge of your page. For a CV, we want them narrow so we have more room.

1
Open Microsoft Word

Create a new Blank Document. Don't use a template — we're building ours from scratch.

2
Click the Layout tab

Look at the tabs at the top of Word: Home, Insert, Draw, Layout… Click on Layout.

3
Click Margins → Narrow

In the Layout tab, click the Margins button. Choose Narrow from the list. Your page edges will get smaller.

4
Save your file right away!

Press Ctrl + S. Name it YourName_CV — for example: Emma_Jones_CV. Save it to your school folder.

• Step 3 — Add Section Headings with Bullet Points

📘 Word Skill: Bullet Points Bullet points make lists easy to read. They're perfect for CVs — especially for Key Skills.

After your name, we'll add the section headings. For now, just type these as bold text — we'll fill them in during the worksheet.

1
Press Enter twice after your name

This gives some breathing room. Change the font size back to 12 first.

2
Type: PERSONAL PROFILE

Make it bold (Ctrl+B), size 14, and left-aligned (Ctrl+L). Press Enter.

3
Leave 3 blank lines, then type: KEY SKILLS

Same formatting — bold, size 14. Press Enter.

4
Now turn on Bullet Points

Click the bullet point button in the Home tab (it looks like ≡ with dots). Type 3 placeholder skills for now — we'll change them later.

Quick shortcut: To turn off bullet points, just press Enter twice, or click the bullet point button again to switch it off.

Tables, Images & Alignment

Follow each step carefully in your Word document.

📘 Word Skill: Tables A table is a grid of rows and columns. We'll use one to display your Education section neatly.

📊 Skill 1 — Insert a Table for Education

We'll create a 3-column table: one column for the school name, one for the subject, and one for the grade.

1
Open your CV Word document

Find the file you saved last lesson: YourName_CV.docx. Double-click to open it.

2
Click below your KEY SKILLS section

Press Enter a couple of times, then type EDUCATION in bold, size 14. Press Enter once.

3
Insert a Table

Click the Insert tab → click Table → move your mouse to select 3 columns, 3 rows → click to insert.

4
Add your headings in Row 1

Click in the first box and type School / College. Tab to the next box: Subject. Tab again: Grade / Predicted. Make all three bold.

5
Fill in your details in Rows 2 and 3

Row 2: your current school name, a subject you study, your predicted grade. Row 3: another subject. Use Tab to move between cells.

6
Format the table borders

Select all cells (click and drag). Right-click → Borders and Shading → choose All Borders. This makes the table look neat.

📘 Word Skill: Inserting Images You can add a photo to the top of your CV. It makes it personal. We'll insert it and then resize it so it fits neatly.

🖼️ Skill 2 — Insert & Resize a Photo

Some CVs include a small photo in the top corner. We'll add one using a free avatar if you don't have a photo ready.

1
Click at the very top of your document

Click just before your name at the top. We want to insert the image here.

2
Insert → Pictures → This Device

Click the Insert tab → Pictures → This Device. Find your photo or a downloaded avatar image.

3
Resize the image

Click the image once to select it. You'll see small squares at the corners — these are handles. Drag a corner handle (not a side one) to make it smaller. Aim for about 3cm × 3cm.

4
Set Text Wrapping to "Square"

Right-click the image → Wrap Text → Square. Now you can drag it to sit in the top-right corner of your name.

No photo? No problem! Go to Insert → Online Pictures and search for avatar silhouette — pick a simple professional one.
Important! Always drag a corner handle to resize — never a side handle. Side handles stretch the image and make it look squashed!
📘 Word Skill: Alignment & Spacing Spacing between sections makes a CV easier to read. Alignment means making sure things line up properly.

📐 Skill 3 — Spacing & Final Alignment

1
Add space between sections

Click at the end of each section. Press Enter once between sections. Don't press it lots of times — we'll use paragraph spacing instead.

2
Use Paragraph Spacing

Select a section heading. Go to Home → Line Spacing (the icon with lines and arrows) → Add Space After Paragraph. Do this for each heading.

3
Check your alignment

Your name: centred. Your section headings: left-aligned. Your body text and bullet points: left-aligned. Use Ctrl+E for centre, Ctrl+L for left.

4
Save one more time!

Press Ctrl+S. Your CV is nearly done!
