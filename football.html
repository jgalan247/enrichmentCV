📋 What Makes a Professional Programme?

Real football clubs spend time and money making their matchday programmes look brilliant. Fans buy them to keep as souvenirs! Here is what makes them look professional:

Key features of a great programme: A big bold title with the club name and match details. A header showing the club name on every page. A footer with page numbers. A numbered squad list (1 = goalkeeper, then defenders, midfielders, forwards). Bullet points for rules, facilities, or information.

In Microsoft Word, you have all the tools to create these features. Let's learn which tools to use!

Word Skills: Titles, Headers & Lists

⚽ Skill A: Create a Bold, Coloured Match Title

The title is the first thing people see. It needs to be big, bold, and eye-catching — like the front of a real matchday programme.

📄 Microsoft Word — Programme.docx
B 36 A▼ ≡ Centre
RIVERSIDE FC
vs City United
Saturday 15th March 2025 — 3:00 PM Kick-off
1
Type your club name

Open a new blank document. Type your club name in CAPITALS, e.g. RIVERSIDE FC

2
Select the text

Triple-click on the club name to select the whole line, or use Ctrl + A

3
Make it bold

Press Ctrl + B or click the B button on the ribbon.

4
Change the font size

With the text selected, change the font size box to 36. You can type the number directly into the size box.

5
Change the colour

Click the Font Colour dropdown (the A with a coloured bar). Choose a dark green or your club colour.

6
Centre the title

Press Ctrl + E to centre the text. Your title should now be big, bold, coloured, and in the middle!

📄 Skill B: Add a Header and Footer

Headers and footers appear on every page of your document. Real programmes always have the club name at the top and page numbers at the bottom.

Where to find it in Word: Go to the Insert tab on the ribbon. Look for the Header & Footer section on the right side. Click Header to add text at the top, or Footer to add text at the bottom.
1
Open the Header

Click Insert → Header → Choose Blank (the first option).

2
Type your club name

In the header area, type your club name. Make it bold and a smaller font size (e.g. 10pt).

3
Add a Footer with page numbers

Click Insert → Footer → Choose Blank. Then click Insert → Page Number → Current Position.

4
Close the header/footer

Double-click on the main document area, or press Esc to close the header/footer editing mode.

📝 Skill C: Numbered Lists & Bullet Points

Your squad list should be numbered (1, 2, 3...) and your rules should use bullet points (•). Word makes this easy!

📄 Programme.docx — Lists
1. 2. 3. • — —
SQUAD LIST
1. T. Williams (GK)
2. J. Henderson
3. M. Carter
4. R. Patel (C)
5. D. Singh
GROUND RULES
• Respect all players and officials
• Arrive 15 minutes before kick-off
• Full kit required including shin pads
1
Start a numbered list

Click the Numbering button (1. 2. 3.) on the Home tab. Type your first player name and press Enter — the next number appears automatically!

2
Type all your players

Keep typing player names and pressing Enter. Word numbers them for you. Press Enter twice to stop the list.

3
Start bullet points

For your rules section, click the Bullets button (• — —) on the Home tab. Type each rule and press Enter.

Keyboard shortcut! You can start a numbered list by typing 1. then Space and Word will automatically switch to a numbered list! For bullets, type * then Space.

📸 Why Do Programmes Need Images and Tables?

Look at any real matchday programme — they are full of photos, club badges, and league tables. These make the programme exciting to read and help fans find important information quickly.

Images — A club badge at the top makes it look official. Action photos make it exciting.
Tables — League tables and fixture lists need rows, columns, and headings to be clear.
Shapes — Divider lines, circles for badges, and coloured boxes for section headers.

Word Skills: Images, Tables & Shapes

Follow each step carefully. Try it in Word as you go!

🖼️ Skill A: Insert and Position Images

Your programme needs at least two images — a club badge and an action photo. Here is how to add them and control where they appear.

📄 Programme.docx — Images
📷 Pictures 📐 Wrap Text 📏 Size
⚽
RIVERSIDE FC
vs City United
📸 Action Photo Here
1
Click where you want the image

Put your cursor where you want the image to appear in your document.

2
Insert the image

Click Insert → Pictures → This Device. Find your image file and click Insert.

3
Resize the image

Click the image. Drag one of the corner handles to make it bigger or smaller. Always use corners — not sides — to keep the image in proportion!

4
Set text wrapping

Click the image → click the Layout Options icon (or go to Format → Wrap Text). Choose Square so text flows neatly around the image. For a badge in the corner, use Tight or In Front of Text.

5
Position the image

Once wrapping is set, you can drag the image to exactly where you want it on the page.

Common mistake! If your image is "In Line with Text" (the default), you cannot drag it freely. Always change the text wrapping first!

📊 Skill B: Create a Fixtures / League Table

Tables are perfect for showing match results, fixtures, and league standings. Let's create one!

📄 Programme.docx — Table
📊 Table 🎨 Table Design 📐 Layout

1
Insert a table

Click Insert → Table. Drag to select 6 columns and 7 rows (1 header row + 6 teams).

2
Type the column headings

In the first row, type: Team, P (played), W (won), D (drawn), L (lost), Pts (points). Make them bold.

3
Fill in the data

Type team names and their stats in each row. Press Tab to move to the next cell.

4
Style the table

Click inside the table. Go to Table Design tab. Choose a style you like — something with shaded headers looks professional.

5
Centre the numbers

Select the number columns. Click Centre Align so the numbers line up neatly.

Keyboard shortcut! Press Tab to move to the next cell in a table. Press Shift + Tab to go back. If you press Tab in the last cell, a new row is added automatically!

🔷 Skill C: Using Shapes for Dividers and Badge Design

Shapes add a professional touch. Use them for section dividers, coloured banners, or even a simple badge design.

1
Insert a shape

Click Insert → Shapes. Choose a shape — try a rectangle for a section divider or a circle for a badge.

2
Draw the shape

Click and drag on your document to draw the shape. Hold Shift while dragging to make a perfect circle or square.

3
Change the colour

Click the shape → Shape Format tab → Shape Fill to change the background colour. Use Shape Outline to change the border.

4
Add text to a shape

Right-click the shape → Add Text. Type your club name or section heading inside the shape!

5
Use a line as a section divider

Insert → Shapes → choose a Line. Draw it across the page between sections. Change its colour and thickness using Shape Outline.
