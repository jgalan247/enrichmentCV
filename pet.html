Lesson 1

Titles, Formatting & Spell Check

Learn the building blocks of an awesome pet information leaflet — headings, formatting and how to catch every spelling mistake!

🎯 Learning Objectives

1 Create a colourful, bold title for your leaflet
2 Add consistent sub-headings with underline and colour
3 Use Spell Check (F7) to find and fix spelling mistakes
4 Use Find & Replace (Ctrl+H) to change words quickly

📋 What Makes a Great Pet Leaflet?

An information leaflet is a document that gives someone useful facts about a topic. For your pet leaflet, you need:

  • A big, colourful title — so people know what the leaflet is about
  • Clear sub-headings — to organise your information into sections
  • No spelling mistakes — so your leaflet looks professional
  • Consistent formatting — same font, same style throughout
  • Pet images — to make it eye-catching and fun
  • A care table — to show how to look after the pet
  • Tip boxes with borders — to highlight important facts

Lesson 1 — Skills

Step-by-Step Word Instructions

Follow these steps carefully to create your pet leaflet title, sub-headings, and fix spelling mistakes.

🎨 Skill A: Creating a Colourful Title

Your leaflet needs a big, bold, colourful title that grabs attention. Here's how to create one:

1
Type your title

At the top of your document, type your pet leaflet title. For example: "My Amazing Golden Retriever" or "Everything You Need to Know About Hamsters"

2
Select the title text

Click and drag to highlight all the title text, or triple-click to select the whole line.

3
Make it big and bold

Change the font size to 24pt or larger using the size dropdown. Press Ctrl+B to make it bold.

4
Add colour

Click the Font Colour dropdown (the "A" with a coloured line underneath) in the Home tab. Choose a colour that stands out but is still easy to read.

5
Centre it

Press Ctrl+E to centre your title on the page.

What it should look like in Word Your title should be big, bold, colourful and centred at the top of the page. It should immediately tell the reader what the leaflet is about.

📑 Skill B: Adding Sub-Headings

Sub-headings break your leaflet into clear sections. They should all look the same (consistent formatting).

1
Plan your sections

Good sections for a pet leaflet: "Why They Make Great Pets", "Fun Facts", "How to Care for Them", "Top Tips"

2
Type and format each sub-heading

Type your sub-heading, select it, then make it: Bold (Ctrl+B), Underlined (Ctrl+U), Size 14-16pt, and a matching colour.

3
Keep them consistent!

Every sub-heading should look exactly the same — same size, same colour, same bold + underline. This makes your leaflet look professional.

Pro Tip: Use Format Painter! Format one sub-heading perfectly, then double-click the Format Painter (paintbrush icon) and click on your other sub-headings to copy the formatting instantly!

🔍 Skill C: Using Spell Check

Spelling mistakes make your leaflet look careless. Word helps you find and fix them!

1
Look for squiggly lines

Red squiggly lines = spelling error. Blue squiggly lines = grammar error. These appear automatically as you type.

2
Right-click to fix

Right-click on any underlined word and Word will suggest the correct spelling. Click the right one to fix it!

3
Run the full Spell Check

Press F7 to open the Spelling & Grammar checker. This goes through your WHOLE document and finds every mistake. Click Change to fix each one.

🔄 Skill D: Find & Replace

If you've spelled a word wrong in lots of places, Find & Replace can fix them all at once!

1
Open Find & Replace

Press Ctrl+H to open the Find and Replace dialog box.

2
Type the wrong word

In the "Find what" box, type the misspelled word. For example: hamstar

3
Type the correct word

In the "Replace with" box, type the correct spelling. For example: hamster

4
Replace All

Click "Replace All" and Word will fix every single one! A message will tell you how many replacements were made.

Lesson 2

Images, Tables & Borders

Make your pet leaflet look amazing with photos, a care schedule table, and colourful tip boxes!

🎯 Learning Objectives

1 Insert at least 2 images with text wrapping
2 Create a pet care schedule table
3 Add borders and shading to tip/fact boxes
4 Check your formatting is consistent throughout

📸 Why Do Leaflets Need Images and Tables?

An information leaflet without images is like a pet without a name — it just doesn't feel right! Images make your leaflet eye-catching and help the reader understand your content.

Tables are perfect for organising information neatly. A pet care schedule table lets the reader see quickly what needs doing and when.

Borders and shading help you create "tip boxes" and "fact boxes" that draw the reader's eye to the most important information.

Lesson 2 — Skills

Step-by-Step Word Instructions

Follow these steps to add images, tables, and borders to your pet leaflet.

📸 Skill A: Inserting Pet Images

Your leaflet needs at least 2 images of your pet. Here's how to insert them and make them look great:

1
Find your images

Search online for images of your pet. Right-click and choose "Save image as..." to save it to your computer. Save at least 2 images.

2
Insert the image

In Word, click Insert tab → Pictures → This Device. Find your saved image and click Insert.

3
Resize the image

Click on the image and drag the corner handles (the small circles at the corners) to make it smaller. Hold Shift while dragging to keep it in proportion!

4
Set text wrapping

Click the image, then click the small Layout Options icon that appears (or go to Format tab → Wrap Text). Choose "Square" so text wraps neatly around the image.

5
Position the image

Now you can drag the image anywhere on the page! Place one near the top of your leaflet and another further down.

Common Mistake! If you don't set text wrapping, the image will push your text down and leave big ugly gaps. Always set wrapping to "Square" or "Tight".

📊 Skill B: Creating a Pet Care Table

A care schedule table organises your pet care information neatly. Here's how to create one:

1
Insert a table

Click Insert tab → Table → drag to select 4 columns and 5 rows (or more if you need them).

2
Add the column headings

In the first row, type these headings: Task, How Often, Time, Notes. Make them bold.

3
Fill in the care tasks

Add care tasks like: Feeding, Walking, Brushing, Cleaning cage/tank, Vet visits. Fill in how often, how long, and any notes.

4
Format the header row

Select the top row, then go to Table Design tab. Add shading (a background colour) to the header row to make it stand out. Use a colour that matches your leaflet theme.

🔲 Skill C: Borders & Shading for Tip Boxes

Tip boxes and fact boxes make important information stand out. Here's how to create them:

1
Type your tip text

Type something like: "🐾 Top Tip: Golden Retrievers need at least 2 hours of exercise every day!"

2
Select the text

Highlight all the text you want in the tip box.

3
Add shading (background colour)

Go to Home tab → click the Shading button (paint bucket icon) → choose a light colour like pale purple or pale yellow.

4
Add a border

Go to Home tab → click the Borders dropdown arrow → choose "Outside Borders". This adds a box around your text!

This is what a tip box looks like! You're looking at one right now. It has a light background colour (shading) and a border around it. In Word, you can create the same effect using the Shading and Borders buttons.

🔍 Skill D: Final Formatting Check

Before you finish, check that your entire leaflet is consistent. Ask yourself these questions:

Consistency Checklist
  • Are all sub-headings the same size, colour and style?
  • Is the body text all the same font and size?
  • Are images a similar size with text wrapping set?
  • Do tip boxes all have the same shading and border style?
  • Have you run Spell Check (F7) one final time?
  • Is there a clear gap between each section?
